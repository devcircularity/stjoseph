About Us

Empowering Nairobi's Underserved Communities

Kenya's Nairobi city, home to approximately 5 million people, has over 54.7% of its population living in informal urban settlements and slums, covering about 10% of the city's residential area. These areas, often overlooked in development plans, face critical issues such as lack of public schools, rise in child labor, unemployment, and more. Our Programs

SJWCC's programs aim to tackle the critical issues faced by impoverished communities in Nairobi, with a focus on empowering children, youth, and women through sustainable development goals (SDGs).

Education and Training

Providing accessible educational opportunities and vocational training to enhance skills, literacy, and employability.

Nutrition Based Feeding Program

Offering nutritious, balanced meals to support the health and well-being of children and families in need.

Needs Assessment and Empowerment

Identifying the critical needs within communities to tailor support and empowerment programs effectively.

Women and Youth Empowerment

Engaging women and the youth in empowerment programs to promote equality, economic independence, and social inclusion.

Creativity and Talent Development

Fostering creative expression and talent among children and youth through arts, sports, and cultural activities.

Our Objectives

Guided by a commitment to sustainable development and empowerment, SJWCC focuses on achieving the following objectives to support and uplift communities in need.

Inclusive and Equitable Education

Ensure access to inclusive and equitable quality education and promote lifelong learning opportunities for all, focusing on the most vulnerable and marginalized groups.

Support for Orphans and Vulnerable Children

Provide special attention and support to orphans, children with disabilities, and those facing learning difficulties to ensure they benefit from educational systems.

Nutrition and Well-being

Implement a nutritious and well-balanced feeding program to address malnutrition and promote the overall well-being of children and families.

Women and Youth Empowerment

Empower women and the youth by providing them with opportunities for personal and professional development, thus fostering economic independence and social empowerment.

Community Needs Assessment

Conduct thorough needs assessments to identify and address the specific challenges faced by children, youth, women, and people living with disabilities in the communities we serve.

Frequently Asked Questions

Discover more about how you can contribute to changing lives through our various educational support programs.

1How can I sponsor a child?

You can sponsor a child by choosing from our list of needy pupils, available upon request. We offer both full and partial sponsorship options covering tuition, meals, and uniforms.

2Can I visit the child I sponsor?

Yes, we encourage sponsors to meet their sponsored child. We can arrange visits to the school or the child's home, or organize virtual meetings for sponsors who cannot visit in person.

3How are the children selected for the programme?

Children are selected based on assessments by our social workers, who visit homes to identify those in the most need. We prioritize children from the most economically challenged backgrounds.

4What does full sponsorship cover?

Full sponsorship covers the child's tuition fees, school uniforms, and meals. It ensures that the child receives comprehensive support for their education.

5How are funds managed to ensure transparency?

We maintain the highest level of transparency and accountability in fund management. Donors receive regular updates, including academic performance reports and financial expenditures related to their sponsored child.

6Can I contribute in ways other than financial sponsorship?

Absolutely. We welcome volunteers for various activities, donations of books, uniforms, or even your expertise in educational support, healthcare, and other areas that benefit the children and the community.
